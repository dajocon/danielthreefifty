Hannah Lee is a symbolic composite of Hannah from the Netflix series *Dark* and Lee from the John Steinbeck novel *East of Eden*. In Dark, Hannah has an unhealthy infatuation with a male character that leads her down dark paths. In East of Eden, Lee is best known for his credo "Thou mayest," repesenting our ability to overcome temptation. Taken together, in this short poem, the name represents the fear of compromising a relationship with God for something or someone temporal, but with the hopeful cry of Lee added upon it, resulting in the glorification of God. 

*From the temptation to love the temporal more than the eternal, deliver us* 

Hannah Lee \
How much time is left to be \
Here with me?

Pretty, pretty all too soon \
We all see our last full moon \
As you ascend up to Three

Father, into your hands I commend my spirit \
Thanks for the grace you gave me to inherit \
Always giving new life \
The promise of eternal life \
For my God given wife
